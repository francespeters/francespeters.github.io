Project Overview

Through SCOUT – Northeastern’s Student Design Collective, our team partnered with The Student Kitchen to design and build a cohesive digital presence that clearly communicates their mission, improves usability for partners and donors, and establishes a clear visual identity. As Design Lead, I oversaw the full design process from research and brand development to high-fidelity UI and front-end implementation. I worked closely with both the client and developers to deliver a polished, mission-driven website that aligned with the brand’s personality.

Competitor Research

We analyzed websites of comparable nonprofit and food-access organizations to identify common usability patterns, content structures, and branding approaches. This research informed decisions around navigation clarity, storytelling hierarchy, and calls-to-action, ensuring The Student Kitchen’s site felt both credible and approachable while standing out within the nonprofit space.

Brandbook

I created a comprehensive brand book for The Student Kitchen, defining typography, color palette, logo usage, and visual guidelines. This system established a consistent and recognizable identity that could scale across digital and print touchpoints beyond the website.

Design System

A modular design system was built in Figma to support consistency and efficiency across screens. Components, spacing rules, and reusable patterns enabled smooth collaboration between designers and developers and allowed for easy future updates.
