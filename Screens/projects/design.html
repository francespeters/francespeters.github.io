Typographic Grids

For this project, I used InDesign to explore typographic design by creating six unique compositions from a single text using a predefined grid. I focused on interpreting the meaning of the text and translating it visually through typographic hierarchy. This involved careful consideration of alignment, placement, tracking, leading, size, case, and style, as well as the strategic use of negative space to enhance readability and visual impact. Each composition presents a distinct approach, demonstrating how typographic choices can shape the viewer’s experience of the text.

Album Design

For this project, I selected a song I enjoy and worked with its lyrics to design a 7″ vinyl record cover. I began by creating a classical, readable composition of the lyrics in a 7 x 7-inch square, focusing on proper paragraph formatting, text alignment, legible typefaces, and thoughtful use of negative space. The song title was emphasized through hierarchy using size, placement, and style. I then produced five interpretative typographic compositions, experimenting with type size, placement, alignment, and style to convey the song’s meaning and emotion
