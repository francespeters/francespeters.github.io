MOHAI MAP REDESIGN

June 2024 - August 2024

UX Design | Prototyping | Usability Studies

Disciplines: UI/UX Design | User Flow Mapping | User Research | Prototyping | Wire Frames

Tools: Figma | Adobe Photoshop

Project Overview

This project was the final capstone for my User Experience and Design Program in Seattle, WA. The program provided a foundation in Human-Computer Interaction and industry-standard design practices while connecting students with real-world sponsors seeking design improvements.

My team of four collaborated with the Museum of History & Industry (MOHAI) in Seattle to address challenges with their museum exhibit navigation. The museum sought a more cohesive way to integrate their existing static maps into a user-friendly interface.

Over six weeks, I led my team in an evidence-based, user-centered design process, resulting in a functional prototype that represented our sponsor’s needs.

User Research

The design process started with interviewing 5 museum goers to better understand the current limitations of the museum’s static map design and identify opportunities for improvement.

Ideation & Lo-Fidelity Prototyping

Our team brainstormed solutions utilizing common design practices such as storyboarding, user flow mapping, and quick wireframes. We started to refine our design with low-fidelity prototypes.

High-Fidelity Prototyping

Our high-fidelity prototype was designed using Figma. To integrate preexisting maps from the museum Adobe Photoshop was used to edit and adapt the maps while preserving key information.

Usability Testing

We conducted four usability tests to evaluate our design, identify weaknesses, and establish goals for future iterations.
