Project Overview

NextStop AR was designed in Figma to explore an innovative approach to navigating train stations after recognizing the unintuitive design of the Boston T. The interactive prototype leverages augmented reality to overlay real-time wayfinding cues, helping users seamlessly navigate complex station layouts. My teammates an I aimed to address the difficulty of navigating the T stations via the current signage by designing a mobile app that allows users to follow AR arrows in the station. This mobile app combines the physical world with visual cues (arrows) to guide users through confusing stations. It not only appeals to tourists who have never used the T, but also to regular commuters that are traveling a new traine line. The design focuses on accessibility and clarity, ensuring a more intuitive transit experience.

Problem Area

Navigating public transit systems can be confusing, especially for a tourist that is new to a city.
Common challenges include:

  • - Lack of clear signage

  • - Confusing station layouts

  • - Lack of universal vocabulary

  • - Unfamiliar terminology

  • - Unsanitary environments

  • - Overcrowded stations

  • - Language barriers

  • - Accessibility issues for individuals with disabilities

  • - Unreliable arrival times

  • - Unclear payment information

Identifying Users

Who are the main users of the Boston T?

  • - Tourists

  • - Students

  • - Everyday commuters/residents

How do we prioritize accessibility?

  • - Alternative routing

  • - Accounting for language barriers

  • - Clear visual cues
